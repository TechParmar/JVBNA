Introduction to Jainism

Jainism is one of the oldest religions of world. It believes in a cyclical nature of universe. Thus the universe is beginning less and ending less.

The wheel of time incessantly revolves like a pendulum. In the first half circle it revolves from the descending to the ascending stage where human prosperity, happiness, and life span increases and in the second half circle it revolves from the ascending stage to the descending stage where prosperity, happiness, and life span decrease. Now we are in fifth era of the second half circle, i.e. descending time cycle.

Rishabhdev, was the first Tirthankar of this cycle of time and the last of the 24 Tirthankaras was Lord Mahavir who lived between 599 and 527 B.C.E. Tirthankar means Maker of the four fold congregation consists of monks, nuns, lay men and lay women. In ancient times Jainism was known by many names such as the religion of Shraman, Nirgantha. Later on it has been known as Jain Dharma.

Samani

Jayant Pragya Ji


What a paradox, when most humans in their thought process long for peace, that very mind get controlled by one’s ego and hatred, giving rise to uncompromising sectarianism and intolerance, which in-turn results in a violent world. According to Acharya Mahapragya, “All major religions express a very similar spiritual and moralistic view. The difference is mainly in the ritualistic aspect of the different religions. Let us with magnanimity give each individual the religious independence in its ritualistic activities. But let us all work together towards our personal growth as more enlightened Human Beings.”

The wisdom of Jain Scriptures states that the independence of every individual depends on the well-being of the entire environment. For that we should be aware of the respect for every individual, our family, our neighbors and the community of living beings and a step further for the preservation of ecology - the Earth, the Air, the Water, and all the other Natural Resources.

We must treat others as we wish others to treat us. We make a commitment to respect life and dignity, individuality and diversity, so that every person is treated humanely, without exception.

Our internal conflict between our conscience and our emotions can only be resolve with an inner transformation, where conscience wins over emotions.

Preksha meditation

The word Preksha is derived from the root iksha, which means 'to see'. When the prefix ‘Pra is added, it becomes Pra+iksha=Preksha , which means 'to perceive carefully and profoundly' Here, 'seeing' does not mean external vision, but careful concentration on subtle consciousness by mental insight.

Get Involved!

Jain Vishwa Bharati of North America, by virtue of its permanent center in Iselin, New Jersey, is able to host Samanijis year round, who provide you with spiritual guidance and promote the Jain way of life. Such a center dedicated to your well-being has to be funded to carry out different activities.

Hundreds of adults as well as kids get great benefits of the programs like Gyanshala, Preksha Meditation Camps, Paryushan and Das Laxan (18 days long auspicious celebrations), etc. especially due to presence of Samanijis. Our kids have acquired a very good knowledge of Jainism and we are proud of them.

Don't we all owe back some thing to our society for what we and our kids receive? The benefits we and our kids receive, ask us to contribute our share to society and help JVBNA to reach thousands more. And best thing, when you contribute for this great cause, you get tax benefits as JVBNA is IRS approved "Not for Profit organization" and contributions to JVBNA are tax deductible.

JVBNA has various membership levels such as supporting, sponsor, life, founder, patron and grand patron. Please choose a level suitable to meet your donation desires and call the center.

JVBNA also has Tithi Yojna program to maintain the Center operation for one day. You select the day(s) of your choice (like a birthday or anniversary). By planning in advance, you can take the gochari laabh for the day(s) you select.

No contribution is too small, and we welcome any amount that you want to donate. Please show us your support by becoming a member and donating generously. All your donations are tax deductible.
